Banitsa

Description

Banitsa is a savory Bulgarian cheese pie that is made with just a few ingredients. This dish is made so often because it is simple, easy, and delicious! Banitsa becomes incredibly crispy and flaky after being baked in the oven and is at its prime when it's warm, making it a perfect breakfast! Banitsa can be baked in various ways such as in its form or adding extra ingredients to eleveate the dish. For example it's common to add spinach or even pumpkin for a sweeter dish. My mother-in-law makes banitsa every week for a mid day snack.

If you look into the culture in Bulgaria, you'll find that this dish is often made for celebration or to spread messages. Lucky Charms will be placed in the dish to celebrate the new year or messages will be place inside aluminium. My mother-in-law is Bulgarian and she has made Banitsa full of messages that translate to; baby soon, good fortune, and great success. This dish brings families together and warms our stomaches, but watch out! It's calaorie packed and addictive!

Ingredients

For a beautiful layered Banitsa you will need these items:

  • Filo pastry
  • Feta cheese
  • Eggs
  • Yogurt
  • Oil or Butter
  • Baking Soda

Steps

  1. First we have to make a yogurt mixture adding your yogurt, eggs, baking soda, and oil. Wisk them togthere until well mixed.
  2. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees and oil a large non-stick baking pan.
  3. Carefully, unroll your thawed filo pastry and separate 5 layers. Lay the fist piece down and spread about 1/4 of your yogurt mixture all over it.
  4. Evenly spread 1/4 cup of feta chesse over the yogurt mixture on the first filo sheet.
  5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 with next 3 layers of filo pastry.
  6. Lay the 5th filo sheet on top and cover it with the rest of your yogurt mixture.
  7. Bake for about 30-35 minutes or untill golden brown. When you cut into the banitsa it should not be runny.
  8. Cover your freshly baked banitsa with a lid until it is cooled. The steam will soften the Banitsa so the crisp will be perfect!
